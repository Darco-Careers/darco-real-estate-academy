Advertising & Attracting Prospects

Lesson 1

The Leasing Consultant's Role in Marketing

At a small independent property, the landlord might write their own ads and post them on Craigslist. At the large institutional apartment communities where most leasing consultants work, the marketing operation is more structured — but your role inside it is just as important.

Large management companies typically have a centralized marketing team that manages the community's presence on major listing platforms, runs paid advertising campaigns, and sets the brand standards. As a leasing consultant, your job intersects with marketing in three key ways:

Your Responsibility

Responding to Leads

Every inquiry that comes through — phone, email, online chat, text — is a lead generated by marketing. Your speed and quality of response is what converts that lead into a showing.

Your Responsibility

Updating Availability

Keeping the leasing software and listing platforms current with accurate unit availability, pricing, and move-in dates. An outdated listing wastes everyone's time and damages the community's reputation.

Your Responsibility

Community Presence

How you answer the phone, how the leasing office looks, how the model unit is staged — these are all extensions of the community's marketing. You are the last mile of every marketing dollar spent.

Company Handles

Platform Campaigns

Paid advertising on Apartments.com, Zillow, and other ILS platforms is typically managed by a marketing coordinator or regional team. You work within the system they have built.

💡 Why This Matters to Your Performance

Your leasing numbers — tours booked, applications received, leases signed — are directly tied to how well you respond to the leads that marketing generates. A community with great marketing and a slow-responding leasing consultant will lose prospects to competitors. You are the conversion engine. Marketing gets people to the door; you get them to sign.

Lesson 2

The Platforms — Where Prospects Come From

Apartment prospects today search for housing across multiple platforms simultaneously. Understanding where your leads come from helps you respond appropriately and gives you insight into what prospects have already seen before they contact you.

Internet Listing Services (ILS) — The Primary Platforms

ILS platforms are the dominant source of apartment leads for large communities. Your employer will likely be listed on most of these, and your property management software will integrate with them to sync availability and pricing automatically.

Highest Volume

Apartments.com

The largest dedicated apartment listing platform in the US. Owned by CoStar Group. Most institutional communities invest heavily here. Prospects can filter by price, size, amenities, and neighborhood. Many leads come pre-qualified because of the filtering tools.

High Volume

Zillow Rentals

Part of the Zillow ecosystem — massive consumer traffic. Many prospects use Zillow to search both for-sale and for-rent simultaneously, which means they may be comparing buying vs. renting. Leads tend to be earlier in the decision process.

Growing Fast

Zumper

A strong apartment search platform especially popular with younger renters. Mobile-first design with instant apply functionality. Particularly strong in urban markets like Los Angeles. Prospects can apply directly through the platform.

Strong in LA

HotPads / Trulia Rentals

Both owned by Zillow Group. HotPads has strong presence in urban markets. Trulia drives significant rental traffic. Leads from these platforms often overlap with Zillow leads — same parent company, different audience.

Supplemental

Craigslist

Still relevant in LA, particularly for prospects seeking smaller or independent properties. Large institutional communities often post here as well. Leads from Craigslist tend to be more price-sensitive and require slightly more pre-screening effort.

Social

Facebook Marketplace

Growing rapidly as a rental search tool. Many prospects browse Facebook Marketplace alongside traditional ILS platforms. Strong for word-of-mouth sharing — a prospect can easily share a listing with friends or family who are also looking.

Your Property Management Software and the ILS

At large apartment communities, your property management platform — typically Yardi, Entrata, or RealPage — syncs with the ILS platforms automatically. When a unit becomes available, the system pushes the listing to Apartments.com, Zillow, and other connected platforms with the current price and availability. When a prospect clicks "Contact" on any of these platforms, the lead flows into your CRM within the software.

📋 Know Your Software

When you start a new leasing position, one of your first priorities is to learn how your employer's property management platform handles leads. Where do new inquiries appear? How quickly does the system alert you? How do you log your follow-up activity? A leasing consultant who knows their software moves faster, follows up more consistently, and converts more leads. Ask your property manager for a walkthrough on your first week.

Lesson 3

Writing a Listing That Converts

At large institutional communities, your marketing team writes and manages the primary listing descriptions. But leasing consultants are frequently asked to write or update listings for specific units, respond to inquiry messages, or update availability notes. Knowing how to write a strong listing makes you more valuable — and prevents Fair Housing violations.

The goal of a listing is to give the prospect enough information to self-qualify and take the next step — scheduling a tour. Describe the property and its benefits. Never describe the ideal tenant.

The Anatomy of a Strong Apartment Listing

What every effective listing includes

In order — from most to least important for the prospect's decision

1
Headline — unit size, key feature, location

Lead with what matters most: beds, baths, and location. "Spacious 2BR/2BA in Koreatown — In-Unit Laundry & Parking" answers the two biggest questions immediately.

2
Price and availability

State the monthly rent and available date clearly. Prospects who cannot afford the rent or cannot meet your timeline will self-screen out — which saves your time and theirs.

3
Unit features — specific and descriptive

Hardwood floors, stainless appliances, updated kitchen, private balcony, walk-in closet. Be specific. "Updated kitchen" is stronger than "nice kitchen." Specific details signal a well-maintained property.

4
Community amenities

Pool, gym, rooftop deck, gated parking, doorman, dog run, EV charging. These differentiate your community from individual landlord listings. Prospects searching ILS platforms specifically value community amenities.

5
Location and neighborhood features

Proximity to transit, restaurants, parks, grocery stores — described as neutral location features, not as attributes targeted at any type of resident. "Walking distance to Metro and Whole Foods" is compliant. "Perfect for the professional on the go" is not.

6
Lease terms and pet policy

State whether pets are allowed, any pet restrictions, and the lease term (12-month minimum, etc.). Pre-qualifying on these points eliminates time-wasting inquiries from prospects who cannot meet your requirements.

7
Clear call to action

"Schedule a tour today" or "Apply online at [link]." Tell the prospect exactly what to do next. Do not make them guess.

Before & After — Fair Housing Compliant Listing Language

❌ Violation — Do Not Use
"Beautiful 2BR in quiet, mature neighborhood. Perfect for working professionals or empty nesters. Minutes from top schools and churches. No children please. Serious applicants only."
Violations: "mature neighborhood" (age/familial status), "working professionals or empty nesters" (familial status, age), "churches" (religion), "No children please" (familial status — explicit violation). Every sentence has a problem.
✓ Compliant — Use This
"Bright 2BR/1BA with hardwood floors, updated kitchen, and private patio. In-unit washer/dryer. Assigned parking included. Walk to Silver Lake Reservoir, coffee shops, and Metro Line 2. 12-month lease. Cat-friendly. Income 3× rent required."
Describes the property, its features, and its location. States lease terms and an income requirement that pre-qualifies prospects. No language targeting any demographic. Open to all qualified applicants.

Lesson 4

Photography — Your Listing's First Impression

The majority of prospects on ILS platforms will not click on a listing without photos — and will not schedule a tour based on poor photos. At large communities your marketing team likely handles professional photography for model units. But leasing consultants are often asked to photograph newly available units quickly, and knowing how to take a strong photo makes a real difference in how fast a unit leases.

☀️

Shoot in Natural Light

Open all blinds and curtains. Turn on every interior light. Shoot mid-morning when sunlight is at its best. Dark photos make units look smaller and less appealing than they are.

🧹

Stage Before You Shoot

Remove cleaning supplies, personal items, and anything that should not be in the shot. Close toilet lids. Clear countertops. A tidy unit photographs dramatically better than an identical unit that is slightly cluttered.

🚪

Shoot From the Doorway

Stand in the doorway of each room and shoot toward the back corner. This gives the widest angle and makes rooms appear larger. Avoid shooting from the center of a room — it compresses the space.

📍

Cover Every Room

Living room, kitchen, every bedroom, every bathroom, balcony or patio, and parking if it is included. Prospects who cannot see all rooms in photos assume the missing rooms are the problem.

🏊

Include Community Amenities

Pool, gym, rooftop, lobby, dog run — these photos differentiate your community from individual landlord listings. They justify the rent premium and attract the prospect who is choosing between communities.

🏙️

Exterior on a Clear Day

A clean, well-lit exterior photo builds trust before the prospect even sees the unit. Shoot the building façade and entrance on a day with blue sky if possible. First impressions start before the tour.

✅ The Model Unit Standard

Most large apartment communities maintain a furnished model unit specifically for photography and tours. Keep the model unit in photo-ready condition at all times — blinds open, lights on, surfaces cleared, fresh flowers or a bowl of fruit on the counter. When a prospect walks in to see what their future home could look like, the model unit is your most powerful sales tool. Treat it accordingly.

Lesson 5

Lead Response — Speed Converts

A prospect who submits an inquiry on Apartments.com at 7pm on a Tuesday is likely also contacting two or three other communities at the same time. The first leasing consultant to respond with a helpful, human reply is the one who gets the tour scheduled. Speed is the single biggest driver of lead conversion — more than listing quality, more than amenities, more than price.

Response Time Benchmarks

Under 1 hr
Excellent — highest conversion rate
Prospects are still actively comparing options. A fast, personal response sets you apart immediately and dramatically increases the likelihood of a scheduled tour.
1–4 hrs
Good — still very competitive
Most prospects are still engaged within the first few hours. A clear, helpful response that answers their questions and invites them to schedule a tour will still convert well.
Same day
Acceptable — conversion starts to drop
By end of day, some prospects have already scheduled tours elsewhere. You can still convert but you are playing catch-up. Always aim to respond before the business day ends.
24+ hrs
Poor — most prospects have moved on
A 24+ hour response time signals to the prospect that the community is poorly managed. Many will have already toured and applied elsewhere. Some will not respond to your outreach at all.

What a Strong Lead Response Looks Like

Your first response should accomplish three things: acknowledge the inquiry warmly, answer the most likely questions, and invite them to take a specific next step. Keep it conversational — not corporate.

❌ Weak Response
"Thank you for your interest in our community. Please call our leasing office during business hours at (323) 555-0100 to schedule a showing."
Cold, generic, puts all the work on the prospect. No personality, no helpful information, no sense of urgency. The prospect will call a competitor instead.
✓ Strong Response
"Hi [Name] — thanks for reaching out about the 2BR! We have a unit available on the 4th floor with a city view and in-unit washer/dryer. Rent is $2,450/mo and it's available June 1st. I'd love to show you around — are you available this week? I have openings Thursday at 10am, 2pm, or Saturday morning. Let me know what works!"
Personal, specific, answers the key questions, and offers concrete next steps. Feels like a human wrote it. Much higher likelihood of a scheduled tour.

Tracking Where Your Leads Come From

Professional leasing consultants and their managers track lead sources — which platform generated each inquiry. This data tells the community where to invest marketing dollars and helps you understand your prospect pipeline. Most property management software tracks this automatically when leads flow in through integrated platforms. For walk-ins and phone calls, you should always ask: "How did you hear about us?" and log the answer.

Lead Source Why It Matters What to Ask
Apartments.com / Zillow Confirms your ILS investment is working. High-volume platforms — track cost per lease, not just cost per lead. Already tracked in your PM software via the inquiry form.
Referral from current resident The highest-quality lead source. Referred prospects convert at higher rates and stay longer. Referral programs pay off. "Did a current resident refer you? We'd love to thank them."
Drive-by / walk-in Indicates strong signage and curb appeal. These prospects are already in the neighborhood — very high intent. "Were you just passing by, or have you been looking at our community for a while?"
Google search Organic search traffic. Confirms the community's online presence is working. These prospects did specific research. "How did you find us online — were you searching for apartments in the area?"
Social media Facebook Marketplace, Instagram — growing channel especially for younger renters. Indicates brand awareness. "Did you see us on Facebook or Instagram?"

Module 3 — Key Takeaways

✓

As a leasing consultant at a large community, your primary advertising role is responding to leads, keeping availability current, and converting marketing-generated inquiries into scheduled tours. The marketing team builds the pipeline — you convert it.

✓

The primary ILS platforms are Apartments.com, Zillow Rentals, Zumper, HotPads, and Trulia Rentals. Your property management software (Yardi, Entrata, RealPage) syncs availability to these platforms automatically — learn how your system works on your first week.

✓

Effective listing language describes the property and its features — never the ideal tenant. Any language that signals a preference for or against a protected class is a Fair Housing violation, even if it sounds friendly or positive.

✓

Strong listing photos require natural light, a staged and clean unit, doorway-angle shots, coverage of every room, and community amenity photos. The model unit should always be photo-ready and tour-ready.

✓

Speed of lead response is the single biggest driver of tour conversion. Respond to every inquiry within 1–4 hours when possible. A warm, personal, specific response that answers key questions and offers a concrete next step outperforms generic template responses every time.

✓

Always ask "How did you hear about us?" and track lead sources. This data informs marketing investment decisions and helps your property manager understand where the community's advertising dollars are working.
